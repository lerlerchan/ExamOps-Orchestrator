# Interview Guide — Lecturer / Course Admin Discovery

Purpose
- Validate pain points, frequency, and impact of exam creation workflows. Capture willingness to adopt and potential deployment constraints.

Screening & consent
- Are you responsible for creating or moderating exams for courses? (Yes / No)
- Obtain verbal consent to take notes and anonymize responses.

Context questions
1. Role & context
   - What is your role and how many courses/exams do you manage per year?
   - Which LMS or tools do you currently use (SharePoint, Moodle, Blackboard, MS Teams)?

2. Current workflow
   - Walk me through how you create an exam from syllabus/materials today.
   - How long does it typically take to create a single exam paper? (hours)
   - Who else is involved (moderator, admin, formatting team)?

3. Pain points & priorities
   - What are the most frustrating parts of exam prep? (e.g., aligning to CLOs, formatting, plagiarism checks)
   - How often do formatting or numbering issues require manual fixes?
   - How important is automated CLO/PLO mapping to you? (Not important / Nice-to-have / Critical)

4. Tools & data
   - Do you store syllabus and materials on SharePoint/OneDrive? Any access restrictions?
   - Are templates (e.g., AARO-FM-030) required by policy or faculty?

5. Adoption & constraints
   - What would make you try an automated exam-assistant tool? (time saved, accuracy, easy export)
   - Any regulatory, privacy, or process constraints we should know about?

6. Willingness to pay & pilot
   - Would you participate in a short pilot? If yes, what would success look like?
   - Would the department pay for a hosted solution or prefer self-hosted?

Closing
- Offer to share anonymized findings and pilot results; collect best contact email and any follow-ups.
